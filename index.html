FIRE SAFETY LEGISLATION: THE CURRENT REGIME

Fire Safety provision is scattered among many different pieces of legislation, some dating back as far as the 1700’s. The most important of these are the Fire Precautions Act 1971. which imposed upon certain premises the requirement to obtain a current fire certificate and the Fire Precautions (Workplace) Regulations 1997, which introduced legislation requiring businesses that did not need a fire certificate to conduct an annual fire risk assessment with periodic reviews throughout the year to ensure nothing major had changed since the assessement was made. In 1999 amendments to the latter Bill broadened its scope. In consequence every business employing one or more persons (whether they hold a fire certificate or not) must now undertake regular fire risk assessments.

FIRE SAFETY LEGISLATION: THE NEW ORDER
In May 2004 the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order was laid before Parliament for review and process. The aim of the Bill is to simplify, rationalise and consolidate existing fire safety legislation into one comprehensive law applying to all workplaces and non-domestic premises. It's most significant actions in this respect are to repeal the Fire Precautions Act 1971 and to revoke the Fire Precautions (Workplace) Regulation 1997. Fire risk assessments are at the core of the proposed Order. Under it, business owners will have to make an assessment of the risks from fire not only to employees, but to both occupants and those in the vicinity of their business premises. This Bill has now been fully implementated and requires all who fall within it's scope to fully comply with its requirements.
